In this experiment you are going to see dialogues between Anne and Bob, who are discussing pictures. Each picture shows a number of colored shapes. There are three possible shapes:

Square Circle Triangle

However, only Bob can see the pictures, Ann cannot. So Anne is always asking Bob about what he sees.

In the experiment, you will see a dialogue between Anne and Bob, and the picture that they are talking about. From the dialogue you will only see the answer Bob gave. Supposing Bob described the picture correctly, you will have to guess what Anne's question was – given Bob’s answer and the picture that only he sees. You will have to type in your guess about Anne’s question.

We are interested in your spontaneous responses, so don't think too long before answering.

The experiment begins with a few practice trials to familiarize you with the tasks. The practice trials are about different shapes: moons and stars.
